Roman Numerals

Background

The Romans were active in trade and commerce, and from the time of learning to write they needed a way to indicate numbers.

The system they developed lasted many centuries, and still sees some specialized use today. For example the BBC uses Roman numerals to date their programmes.

Roman Numerals

Basics

Symbol I V X L C D M
Value 1 5 10 50 100 500 1,000

Roman Numerals

Basic Rules

  • Generally, symbols are placed in order of value, starting with the largest values. When smaller values precede larger values, the smaller values are subtracted from the larger values, and the result is added to the total.
  • Small-value symbol may be subtracted from any large-value symbol when placed on the left: IV - 4

Roman Numerals

Additional Rules

  • The symbols “I”, “X”, “C”, and “M” can be repeated three times in succession, but no more.
  • “I” can be subtracted from “V” and “X” only.
  • “X” can be subtracted from “L” and “C” only.
  • “C” can be subtracted from “D” and “M” only.
  • “V”, “L”, and “D” can never be repeated or subtracted.

Roman Numerals

Additional Rules (cont')

  • A number written in Arabic numerals can be broken into digits.
  • For example, 1903 is composed of 1 (one thousand), 9 (nine hundreds), 0 (zero tens), and 3 (three units). To write the Roman numerals, each of the nonzero digits should be treated separately.
  • In the above example, 1,000 = M, 900 = CM, and 3 = III. Therefore, 1903 = M CM III.
